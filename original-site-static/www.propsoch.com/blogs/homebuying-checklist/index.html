Homebuying Checklist: Does Your Dream Home Tick All the Boxes?

Written by
Anshika Garg
January 6, 2025 • 10 minutes read

Remember Monica from Friends, obsessing over every tiny detail and surroundings of her apartment? Or Sheldon Cooper from The Big Bang Theory, who had such specific requirements that he even made a contract for his roommate about space usage. These characters might feel extreme but their great attention to detail is something many of us can relate to when it comes to buying a home.

If you’ve ever found yourself analyzing every aspect of a potential space, you're not alone. Buying a home can feel like a nerve-wracking experience. After all, it's one of the biggest investments you'll make in your life and you want to ensure it's a decision you'll be happy with for years to come.

But with so many things to think about – from the perfect location to the right legal paperwork, from property density to hidden plumbing issues – it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. And that’s where this ultimate homebuying checklist comes in. Whether you’re a first-time buyer or a seasoned investor, having a clear, structured plan will help you avoid common pitfalls and make the best decision.

Think of it this way: You wouldn’t go grocery shopping without a list, right? The same goes for buying a home. This checklist is your personalized shopping list for your dream home, ensuring you don’t miss any crucial details along the way.

In this blog, we've shared six key focus areas that every homebuyer should consider. Each section comes with simple, actionable steps and measurements to make sure you’re ticking off every box and making an intelligent decision.

Ready to dive in? Let’s get started.

Your First Homebuying Checklist: Starting with the Perfect Design

When it comes to buying a property, design plays a big role in how you’ll experience living in that space. But we won’t get too caught up in all the technical details just yet. Instead, we’ll discuss what aspects of design matter most—things like space efficiency, ceiling heights, and open areas. let’s see why these elements are crucial when evaluating a property.

Efficiency

    • What to Look For: RERA carpet area – this is the actual, usable space inside the property that you can live in, not including walls, balconies, or external areas. Think of it as the area where you can place your furniture, move around freely, and create your home. 
    • Why It Matters: A higher carpet area means more room for your daily activities, making your home feel more spacious and comfortable.
    • Ideal Measurement: Aim for a carpet area greater than 60%. The more, the merrier!

Floor-to-Ceiling Height

    • What to Look For: The clear height from floor tiles to the finished ceiling.
    • Why It Matters: Higher ceilings can make a room feel more open and airy, enhancing the overall ambience of your living space.
    • Ideal Measurement: A ceiling height exceeding 2.95 meters is preferable.

Project Density

    • What to Look For: The number of units per acre.
    • Why It Matters: Fewer units per acre typically mean less crowding, which offers more privacy and a more serene environment.
    • Ideal Measurement: The lower the number of units per acre, the better.

Location of Services

    • What to Look For: The distance from your home to high-tension (HT) lines, transformer yards, gas tanks, sewage treatment plants, organic waste converters, drains, etc.
    • Why It Matters: Being close to these facilities can impact your living conditions, from noise pollution to air quality.
    • Ideal Measurement: The greater the distance from your home to these services, the better.

Open Space in the Complex

    • What to Look For: Areas not covered by building footprints.
    • Why It Matters: Open spaces contribute to a healthier, more pleasant living environment, offering areas for recreation and relaxation.
    • Ideal Measurement: The more open space, the better.

Your Second Homebuying Checklist: Vastu

If you've heard of Vastu Shastra, you know it’s all about creating balance in the home. But beyond the beliefs, it’s also about the practical elements that affect how your space “feels.” From the direction your entrance faces to room placements, Vastu helps guide the energy flow in your home. Here, we’ll take a look at why these details matter when picking your dream property.

Entrance Facing:

    • What to Look For: Which direction does the main entrance of the house face?
    • Why It Matters: According to Vastu, facing the right direction can bring prosperity and peace to your home. 
    • Ideal Direction: East or North as they are believed to attract positive energy.

Location of Master Bedroom:

    • What to Look For: Where is the master bedroom located?
    • Why It Matters: This placement is believed to bring stability, strength, and good health to the family.
    • Ideal Direction: The master bedroom should be located in the South-West corner of the house. 

Location of Kitchen:

    • What to Look For: Where is the kitchen placed in the house?
    • Why It Matters: The kitchen should be in the South-East corner, as this is associated with the fire element, which is ideal for cooking.

Is the Plot Shape Gomukhi or Shermukhi:

    • What to Look For: Does the plot have a narrow entrance that opens up (Gomukhi) or a wide entrance that narrows down (Shermukhi)?
    • Why It Matters: Gomukhi plots are considered more auspicious as they allow for better energy flow, whereas Shermukhi plots are generally less preferred.

Measurement for Vastu

    • Entrance Facing: Preferred - East or North
    • Master Bedroom: South-West
    • Kitchen Location: South-East
    • Plot Shape: Gomukhi is better

Your Third Homebuying Checklist: Legal & Approvals

No one wants surprises when it comes to paperwork. Legal & approvals are crucial to ensure that everything is in order before you seal the deal. From the RERA sanction plan to ensuring the property has clear titles, we’ll guide you through what approvals to look for so you can confidently move forward with the purchase.

Key Approvals:

    • What to Look For: Check if the property has all the necessary approvals, including the RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) registration, building plans, fire safety NOCs, environmental clearances, and occupancy certificates (for completed projects).
    • Why It Matters: Without these approvals, you may face future legal issues, delays in possession, or complications when reselling the property.

Title Opinion Availability:

    • What to Look For: A legal opinion on the property title from a reputable law firm.
    • Why It Matters: This ensures that the property has a clear title, meaning no one else can lay a claim to the land.

Pending Litigations:

    • What to Look For: Check if there are any ongoing legal cases related to the property or land.
    • Why It Matters: Pending litigations could complicate your ownership or delay possession of the property.

Measurement for Legal Safety

    • Key Approvals: Ensure all necessary approvals and documentation are in place (RERA, Building plans, etc.)
    • Title Opinion: Available from a reputed law firm
    • Pending Litigations: Court search results should show "Nil" encumbrance (clear title)

Your Fourth Homebuying Checklist: Neighbourhoods

You know what they say: location is everything. But when it comes to your neighbourhood, there’s more to consider than just proximity to shops and schools. We’ll check how the vibe, infrastructure, and overall area affect your decision. Because a great home is nothing without a great location, right?

Disturbances

    • What to Look For: Check if there are any disturbances in the neighborhood, such as proximity to industries, religious institutions, slums, or graveyards.
    • Why It Matters: Disturbances can affect your daily life by causing noise, pollution, or an overall lack of peace. The further you are from such areas, the better.

Connectivity

    • What to Look For: Assess the proximity to transportation hubs such as, metro stations, bus stops or distance from the airport.
    • Why It Matters: Good connectivity ensures that you're well-connected to the city, making commuting easier and more efficient.

Access Road

    • What to Look For: Inspect the quality of the main access road to your property, that includes road width, condition, and proper lighting.
    • Why It Matters: A wide, well-maintained road ensures smooth traffic flow and safety, while proper lighting adds security and convenience, especially during the night.

Social Infrastructure

    • What to Look For: Evaluate the availability of essential facilities in the neighbourhood, such as schools, hospitals, malls, shopping centres, parks, etc.
    • Why It Matters: A neighbourhood with good social infrastructure provides convenience and supports a high quality of life for you and your family.

Measurement for Neighbourhood Quality:

    • Disturbances: The higher the distance from industries, graveyards, or slums, the better.
    • Connectivity: The closer the better, especially to metro stations, bus stops, and airports (without compromising on peace).
    • Access Road: Ensure the road is wide, well-lit, and in good condition.
    • Social Infrastructure: The closer essential facilities (schools, hospitals, etc.) are, the better.

Your Fifth Homebuying Checklist: Home Loans 

Understanding your financial options and loan terms can help make the homebuying process smoother. From interest rates to loan tenure, we’ll walk you through the key financial aspects to consider so you can make the right choice.

Property Pre-Approval from the Bank

    • What to Look For: Ensure that you have pre-approval for a home loan from trusted banks like HDFC, ICICI, SBI, and other reputed banks.
    • Why It Matters: Pre-approval gives you a clear idea of how much loan you’re eligible for, streamlining your property search and making your decision easier.

Tenure of Loan

    • What to Look For: Check the loan tenure offered by the bank, which means the number of years you're expected to pay back the loan.
    • Why It Matters: A longer tenure reduces your monthly EMI but increases the overall interest you pay. A shorter tenure may result in higher EMIs but saves you money on interest in the long run.

Interest Rates

    • What to Look For: Compare interest rates across banks, the rate charged by the lender in percentage.
    • Why It Matters: A lower interest rate means you pay less over the life of the loan. Always check for competitive rates to ensure you're getting the best deal.

Processing Fee

    • What to Look For: Find out what fee the bank charges for processing your loan application.
    • Why It Matters: Lower processing fees can reduce the upfront costs of securing your loan.

Measurement for Home Loan Quality:

    • Property Pre-Approval: Lower the better (faster pre-approval process).
    • Tenure of Loan: Shorter tenure saves you money in the long term, but you may have higher monthly payments.
    • Interest Rates: Lower the better (a lower rate means lower payments and interest over time).
    • Processing Fee: Lower the better (a smaller processing fee saves you upfront costs).

Your Sixth Homebuying Checklist: Home Inspection

Lastly, don't skip the home inspection! It's your chance to spot any issues before committing. A little time spent checking things like cracks or plumbing can save you a lot of hassle down the road. 

Here’s a checklist of the key things to look out for during your property inspection:

Wall Dampness

    • What to Look For: Check for any signs of leakage or moisture in the walls. Damp walls can lead to bigger issues like mold growth, weakening of the structure and poor air quality.
    • Why It Matters: Dampness can deteriorate the paint, cause health issues and significantly reduce the value of your home.

Wall Undulations

    • What to Look For: Feel the surface of the walls to check for any unevenness or undulations. These could indicate structural issues or poor-quality construction.
    • Why It Matters: Undulated walls can affect the overall aesthetic and may suggest that the building hasn’t been built with proper care or quality materials.

Tile Hollowness

    • What to Look For: Tap on the tiles and listen for hollow sounds. Hollow tiles may indicate that they were not properly adhered, or there’s underlying damage.
    • Why It Matters: Hollow tiles can crack or break easily, leading to costly repairs down the line.

Rusting of Grills or Bath Fixtures

    • What to Look For: Inspect grills, bath fixtures, and any exposed metal for signs of rust. Rusting indicates long-term exposure to moisture and can impact the safety and usability of these fixtures.
    • Why It Matters: Rust can weaken the metal, making fixtures unstable and less safe to use.

Electrical and Plumbing Checks

    • What to Look For: Ensure all electrical systems and plumbing are functioning well. Check the outlets, wiring, and plumbing fixtures for leaks or exposed wiring.
    • Why It Matters: Faulty wiring and plumbing can lead to serious safety risks and expensive repairs.

Carpet Area

    • What to Look For: Measure the carpet area to ensure you’re getting the usable space you’re paying for, as per the developer's specifications.
    • Why It Matters: Accurate carpet area measurement ensures you’re not paying for unused or non-functional space.

Measurement for Home Inspection Quality:

    • Wall Dampness: Visual inspection for leaks or moisture signs.
    • Wall Undulations: Visual check for unevenness.
    • Tile Hollowness: Soundcheck (tap tiles to listen for hollowness).
    • Rusting of Grills or Bath Fixtures: Visual inspection for rust or corrosion.
    • Electrical and Plumbing Checks: Consult an expert to assess wiring and plumbing safety.
    • Carpet Area: Physical measurement to confirm the usable area.
